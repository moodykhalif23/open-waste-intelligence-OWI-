Why this exists

Built with collectors, not for a demo.

In most growing cities, waste collection runs on guesswork. Trucks sweep fixed routes whether bins are full or empty. Overflow is discovered by complaint. Recyclable value leaks away unpriced, and the people doing the hardest work have the least data to show for it.

A phone photo is the cheapest sensor in waste management. Our anchor pilot is Safi Cleaners and Recyclers, a community-based organisation in Nairobi running two trucks, a tuk-tuk, and handcart crews across roughly 150 bins. Their collectors already stand in front of every bin, every day — OpenWaste turns that moment into evidence: fill levels, materials, illegal dumping, proof of work.

The platform is Apache-2.0 and deliberately operator-agnostic. Whether you run two trucks or two hundred, a municipality or a co-operative, you can deploy the whole stack yourself and own every byte of your data.

How it works

Four steps, one honest pipeline.

Snap

A collector photographs the bin — under twenty seconds, offline-first, synced when signal returns.

Understand

Models classify material and fill level. People are blurred before anything is stored — recognition is never on the menu.

Decide

Bin health ranks what needs collecting today; routes are optimised for whatever you run — trucks, tuk-tuks, or handcarts.

Share

Aggregates — never raw photos or locations — flow to an open data API for counties, researchers, and residents.

What it returns

Impact you can defend in front of a funder.

KES / kg

Recovered-material value, priced per material against a dated local price table.

CO₂e range

Carbon avoided from cited factors — always a range with a method version, never a point estimate.

km / tonne

Route savings measured against a full-sweep baseline — need-driven collection, not fixed schedules.

0–100

A decomposable cleanliness index per area — a trend for communities, never a shame metric for staff.

Nothing here is sold as a carbon offset. Every factor carries a citation, every method a version — and when the data is thin, the dashboard says insufficient data instead of pretending.

The AI, honestly

The intelligence is gated, not claimed.

A gate, not a slogan

No classifier ships below 0.80 macro-F1 on a frozen, held-out golden set. The current 8-material model passed at 0.95 on public data; field calibration on local photos is the next gate, not a footnote.

Licensed data only

Shipped weights train exclusively on CC BY, CC0, and MIT sources. Non-commercial datasets are quarantined to evaluation. Provenance-unknown scrapes are excluded, full stop.

People teach the model

Reviewers see the least-confident predictions first. Confirmations and corrections flow straight back into weekly retraining — the loop is closed, in production, today.

Privacy is a pipeline stage

A person detector runs before storage: faces and bodies are blurred at ingestion, and the pre-blur originals are destroyed within 72 hours. It finds locations, never people.

Privacy & governance

Hard lines, written into the code.

  • The platform never identifies a person. Not as a feature, not as an option.
  • The public API serves aggregates only — ward by week, cells backed by fewer than 3 bins or 20 observations withheld, everything delayed 7 days.
  • A collector can delete any photo they took. It is erased, no questions asked.
  • Every administrative action lands in an append-only audit trail.
  • Operational photos age out on a per-organisation retention clock; the analytics stay, the pixels go.

Built for the field

If it doesn’t work on a handcart route, it doesn’t ship.

Offline first

Reports queue on the phone and sync when signal returns. Low-end Androids are the target hardware, not an afterthought.

Two languages, day one

English and Kiswahili from the first screen, in both the field app and the dashboard.

Any way you collect

Trucks, tuk-tuks, motorbikes, bicycles, handcarts, on foot — the fleet model and route optimiser treat them all as first-class.

Open source

Deploy the whole platform with one command.

Apache-2.0, no strings. make web brings up the database, object store, workers, models, dashboard, and field app on your own hardware — a laptop, a mini-PC in an office, or a VPS.
